Discussion & conclusion

Discussionâ€”our anticipation of the present design

In this page, we discuss our expectation of the structure of the NanoNeedle and their functions, and based on the discussion, we make a conclusion about its future application in the medicine.

As shown in the previous pages, the NanoNeedle should contain the scaffold containing DNA origami p8064 and M13mp18, and the plasmid with gene of the green fluorescent protein. In addition, the scaffold is modified with the aptamer recognizing the glycoprotein on the cell membrane of the E.coli we use.

We expect the NanoNeedle to be able to react with the E.coli cells and thus deliver the plasmid into them. As a result, the needles must have the structure that allows the scaffold to bind the surface of the bacterial cells and penetrate the cell membrane. As we anticipate, the structure and the functions of the needles should have the characteristics following below.

STRUCTURE that we expect

  • 1. he DNA origami should fold in the shape of hexagonal column and form the enclosed top with mesh structure.
  • 2.  For the sake of successful delivery of the plasmid, the plasmid should be really wrapped in the scaffold and stably exist in the scaffold before the needles contact with the target bacteria.
  • 3. The needles that contain plasmids are able to be stable under the condition achievable in the real environment.

FUNCTIONS that we design

The origami should maintain their structure and keep their reactivity with the bacteria but not human cells in the environment of human body. That is, they can attach to the bacterial cells and simultaneously pore through them, and do no harm for human cells. As soon as the membrane is penetrated by one of the needle made up of the origami wrapping plasmid, the plasmid as a carrier can enter the cell of the target bacterium by diffusion through the pore on the membrane. Because of the plasmid containing the target gene, it will present the target product if the plasmid successfully goes into the cell where the origami with this gene attach to.

We use the gene of green fluorescent protein instead of suicide genes to serve as the target gene for the sake of the convenience of the experimental outcome. If the needles succeed in operation, it will enable us to find out the green fluorescent proteins presented in the bacteria actually infected by the NanoNeedle.

Conclusion--the advance of our design & its future work

The advance of our design

In the theory from Langecker et al.(2013)1, the aptamer have specification for different molecules embedded in the cell membrane, we can design the DNA aptamer for different target strains. In addition, we can design the plasmid containing other target genes to be wrapped in the origami. As a result, we conclude that we will be able to redesign the aptamer to recognize some bacteria resulting in some disease, and the plasmid can carry some suicide genes able to kill the pathogens, and the DNA origami can take effect on the pathogens in the human body. That is, we will be capable of designing a NanoNeedle that can specifically kill the harmful bacteria in the human body without poisoning the human cells and troublesome antibiotics antagonism from the pathogens.

Future work

In the near future, we expect that the experiment design of the NanoNeedle will be applied for the clinical medicine and derive the medicine that can effectively fight against eukaryotic pathogens, which may become an alternative to antibiotic therapy in order to achieve the ultimate goal of kill pathogens.
